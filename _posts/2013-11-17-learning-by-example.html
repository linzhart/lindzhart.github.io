--- layout: post title: Learning by Example date: '2013-11-17T20:39:00.002-07:00' author: Lindsay tags: - mommy moments - quotes modified_time: '2013-11-17T20:40:48.801-07:00' thumbnail: http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-oxQgfdEukO8/UmSgcsAOzmI/AAAAAAAABNQ/nAEJKlXDz4g/s72-c/IMG_1549.JPG blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6431381294158046892.post-7006552980269122481 blogger_orig_url: http://inconclusiveexperiences.blogspot.com/2013/11/learning-by-example.html --- So you know, they say kids learn the most by example.
I find this both reassuring and scary.
And it becomes uncomfortably obvious as I watch my kiddos play - Do I really sound like that?


We got the fabulous and amazing "car cart" at the grocery store - but it only had one steering wheel, as the other had been broken off. Naturally, this caused a scuffle.

Child 3: "I get to sit here."

Child 4: "No! I want to drive."

Child 3: "NO! I'm the Mommy! You're the Daddy!"

Child 4: "Oh."

And that settled the debate. The Mommy drives.

And last week Child #4 asked me to play with her.

"I'm the mommy." She announced, picking up a baby doll. 

"I'm the mommy and you are the daddy." She handed me the baby. 

"I'm going to a meeting." And she put on a pair of my heels and left. 


*     *     *     *

Yup. That is exactly what goes on around here.
