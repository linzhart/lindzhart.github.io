--- layout: post title: Raising them right date: '2014-06-29T21:54:00.001-06:00' author: Lindsay tags: - girls - quotes modified_time: '2014-06-29T21:54:50.506-06:00' thumbnail: http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-vpRzTXhGasw/U7De-1BTl6I/AAAAAAAABRY/wAw5npAvVEQ/s72-c/photo+1+(1).JPG blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6431381294158046892.post-7359516895160590822 blogger_orig_url: http://inconclusiveexperiences.blogspot.com/2014/06/raising-them-right.html --- My extremely shy three-year-old had a bad ear infection and we couldn't get in to her regular pediatrician. I was bracing myself for an awful trip, but the on-call doctor was fantastic.

Matching princess aprons
He stayed out of her space and talked to her, and even drew her a flower on a tongue depressor. He managed to check her ears with no fuss at all and that was that.

Two days later she was still carrying around the flower. She was sitting on the floor, staring at her tongue depressor when she looked up at me in sudden realization, "Mom! Boys can be doctors TOO!"

"You're right! Anyone can be a doctor." And it warmed the cockles of my cold feminist heart.
