I'm really going to have to stop calling her "the baby."  She is one-and-a-half, and soon there will be an actual baby around here.  

Of all my kids, her personality seems to have the widest range.  She absolutely ADORES her family and HATES strangers.  She is sweet and funny and cuddly and independent and opinionated.  One-year-olds should not be allowed to care so much about things like shoes -but OH! She does.

I took she and her sister to pick out sandals the other day and she threw a holy fit.  (The nerve of some sales people, offering to measure her sister's feet!)  I could not get her to try on any shoes, though she loves to try shoes on at home.  I ended up grabbing a pair that I thought might fit and heading home.  I couldn't get her to hold still enough to try them on her until she was strapped in her stroller, and then she kicked and twisted so much they fell off.  So I took them back and grabbed another pair (in a better size).  I got those ones on her feet, but she wouldn't walk.  Not even her biggest brother could convince her that she should wear them; and she thinks he hung the moon.  Her old shoes are so small that they give her blisters - maybe I'll just have to let her go barefoot all summer.

Also, she is the instigator.  I took this video the other day at snack-time, but I kind of missed the best part.  She would throw back her head and yell.  First one, than another of her siblings started copying her until she had them all yelling.  The older three would never have been that loud on their own - she is the idea girl.  And for being terrified of strangers, she sure isn't scared of anything else.  She is constantly climbing on everything.


And this is what happens when you turn on the web cam and let her watch herself on the computer.

And dancing with her sister.
