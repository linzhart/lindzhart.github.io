--- layout: post title: date: '2015-09-25T20:28:00.001-06:00' author: Lindsay tags: modified_time: '2015-09-25T20:28:16.875-06:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6431381294158046892.post-7188404778601616043 blogger_orig_url: https://www.blogger.com/comment.g?blogID=6431381294158046892&postID=7188404778601616043 --- Almost without realizing it, I've been waiting for this day ever since I had a child.

And when he was one, I rocked him to sleep, and cried to think of Lily Potter, having to leave her son to be raised by people who did not love him.

And when everyone in his class was reading Harry Potter I told him he couldn't, because I thought it was too scary. But I realized I was just afraid he wouldn't love it as much as I do.

So I decided to trust in the magic of the story, and I gave him the first book. And my eyes filled with tears as he heard the words, "You're a wizard, Harry." for the first time.

And I was both happy and sad when he begged me to stop reading it aloud to him, because he couldn't bear to wait for me while I made dinner and put his baby sisters to bed.

And I was both happy and exasperated when he hid the books in the bathroom, so that he could read them past bedtime without me knowing.

And so he read all the books again and again, just as I have. (and still do)

And we had wonderful discussions about magic, and favorite characters, and movie marathons.

And sometimes I hid the books from him and forced him to go outside. And sometimes we just read together outside.

And there were so many days when I worried about how I would pay for his lunch or school clothes, and I was sure that I would never be able to afford this.

And yesterday he turned 11.

And tomorrow we board the Hogworts Express!
