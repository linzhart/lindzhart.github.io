(The end.  You can move on now if any more detail is scary to you.)

This is the first baby I've delivered with no medication at all, which I wanted to do more because I hate being numb and unable to move around and less for the "naturalness" of it.

Let me first say that I don't feel partcularly empowered by this.  I've read some childbirth books that recomend going without medication so that you could feel like a powerful, strong woman.  I AM a strong, powerful woman. No amount of baby pushing is going to change that one way or another.  So, yes.  I can deliver a baby with no pain medication.  I can also deliver a baby with pain medication.

I'd been taking some herbs that are supposed to help with childbirth (sporadically, because keeping food down and remembering things are not my forte while pregnant).  Mostly raspberry tea which I hate, but I crave it.  I think it helped, but I can't go back and do it differently to get you a more scientific data sample.  My understanding of the herbs is that they work by toning your muscles ahead of time, to make the labor itself easier.
